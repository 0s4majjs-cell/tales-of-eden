He also mentioned, almost in passing, that he’d been SED-4—Special Extermination Division. One of the elite. The kind of people safe zones relied on when something truly monstrous crawled out of the wasteland. Suddenly, his watchful stillness, his efficiency, the pistol always within reach, all of it made more sense.

What didn’t make sense was why he was still out here. A man like that could have gotten a comfortable slot inside a safe zone—training recruits, guarding a council, something. Instead, he lived in a half-buried barn with a dog and a stove. I didn’t ask why. Not yet.

When the fever finally broke and I stopped waking up drenched in sweat, Karl decided I was ready for “lesson one.”

He woke me before dawn. The world outside the window was still blue and half-formed. My body protested as I dragged myself out of bed and pulled on the thickest clothes I had. The cold slapped me in the face when I stepped outside.
